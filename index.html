Class Info

JavaScript Development (JSD) is comprised of 60 hours of in-class instruction spread evenly over 20 classes.

JSD breaks down into four units:

  1. Fundamentals of JavaScript
  2. The Browser and API's
  3. Persisting Data and Advanced Topics
  4. Building and Deploying Applications

In addition to regular homework assignments, students are expected to complete three, standalone projects:

  1. Slackbot
  2. Feedr
  3. Final Project

Graduation Requirements

In order to successfully complete JSD, students must:

  • Submit 80% of homework assignments
  • Attend 80% of classes
  • Successfully complete the course's final project

Communication

Communication between the instructional team and students is paramount for a successful learning environment. Students are strongly encouraged, at their convenience, to reach out to members of the instructional team with any questions.

Slack will serve as the primary tool for communication between students and the instructional team. Before the first class, you will receive an invitation to our Slack room:

https://jsd3.slack.com/

Slack can be accessed through the browser, or, through a native application runs on your machine.

Homework

Homework will be assigned at the end of class every Thursday and is due the following Tuesday. Files and materials for homework will be distributed during class, usually during lab, through Slack. Students will push (upload) their completed homework to their respective Github repositories for review and grading.
